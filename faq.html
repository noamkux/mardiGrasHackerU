Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to the Mardi Gras Festival FAQ page for New Orleans! Here you will find answers to common questions about this famous celebration, including dates, events, traditions, and safety tips. We hope this information helps you make the most of your Mardi Gras experience.

Mardi Gras is a festival that originated in France and is now celebrated in many countries around the world, particularly in New Orleans, Louisiana. The festival is celebrated in the days leading up to Ash Wednesday, which marks the beginning of the Christian season of Lent.

Mardi Gras is celebrated on the day before Ash Wednesday, which falls on a different day each year depending on the date of Easter. However, the festival season in New Orleans typically begins in early January and lasts until the day before Ash Wednesday.

Mardi Gras is a time of revelry and celebration, with parades, parties, and other events taking place throughout the festival season. People dress up in colorful costumes, throw beads and other trinkets to the crowds, and enjoy traditional food and drink.

The beads and other trinkets thrown during Mardi Gras are a tradition that dates back to the early days of the festival. They are said to represent the colors of the festival and to bring good luck to those who catch them.

While Mardi Gras is known for its wild parties and risqué behavior, there are also many family-friendly events that take place during the festival. Some parades and events are specifically geared toward children, and many families enjoy attending Mardi Gras together.

If you're interested in attending Mardi Gras in New Orleans, it's best to plan ahead and book accommodations well in advance, as the festival draws large crowds. You can also check the official Mardi Gras website for information on events, parades, and other activities taking place during the festival.

Mardi Gras is a time to dress up in colorful costumes and wear masks, hats, and other festive accessories. Many people also choose to wear traditional Mardi Gras colors of purple, green, and gold. However, it's important to keep in mind that New Orleans can be quite warm and humid during the festival season, so comfortable and breathable clothing is also recommended.
